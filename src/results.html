Crop Selection and Yield Estimation

Based on the provided weather data, here is some advice for local farmers to optimize their crop planting and yield expectations:

Early August

  • Suitable Crops: Crops that thrive in moderate temperatures and light precipitation, such as wheat, barley, and peas.
  • Expected Yield: Moderate yields due to the occasional rainfall and relatively stable temperatures.

Mid-August

  • Suitable Crops: Crops that prefer warmer temperatures and can tolerate some rain, such as corn, soybeans, and tomatoes.
  • Expected Yield: Good yields due to the combination of adequate heat and moisture.

Late August

  • Suitable Crops: Crops that can withstand cooler temperatures and potential frost, such as leafy greens, root vegetables, and apple trees.
  • Expected Yield: Lower yields due to the cooler weather and increased likelihood of frost.

Additional Considerations

Besides the weather data, farmers should also consider factors such as soil conditions, availability of water resources, and market demand when selecting their crops and estimating yield.

By combining weather data analysis with other relevant information, farmers can make informed decisions that maximize their productivity and income.
